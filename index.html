Who I Am

The AMS exists to serve students, period. But we can't do that without your buy-in. The AMS saw a voter turnout of 6.9% last year with 4 out of 5 executives running uncontested. Voter turnout as a whole is decreasing.


When it comes to AMS representing student financial interests, students continue to be more likely to disagree (33%) than agree (27%) as well as when trusting AMS to spend their money wisely (37% disagree, 25% agree).


56% of undergraduate students are satisfied with their UBC experience, a significant drop from the 70% satisfaction rate over the past five years. To make matters worse, the pandemic has left many of us feeling isolated, lost, and disconnected from regular university life.


I wish that was the extent of it. We are also in a climate emergency, faced with extreme wealth inequality, and continued oppression of Indigenous, Black, and people of colour. Students are currently faced with so many different obstacles - but we can get through it together. The VP Academic and University Affairs should always prioritize student needs first. I'm running to do exactly that - on a platform centered on compassion, care, and community.


Over the past year, I have gained valuable experience as a student staff in the AMS - mainly in strategy, governance, and policy. I have worked on many different projects, including a huge revision of our Sexual Violence and Respectful Community and Workplace Policies, the AMS Strategic Plan, and a governance review of AMS Council to ensure we are working efficiently and effectively, while maintaining accountability and transparency with the student body. Through this work, I have also seen many of the gaps that currently exist within the AMS and with our relationship with the student body. I also spent two years at UBC outside of the AMS, which allows me to view these issues through the lens of an average student. I also have leadership experience in various areas - as the first ever Diversity and Equity Coordinator for UBC Improv, I am honored to say that I have been able to champion our equity efforts internally and externally, while creating space for marginalized folks in theater and performance at UBC.


I have always been passionate about grassroots organizing and advocacy - as a woman of colour, I have first-hand experience and a deep connection to many of the issues I will advocate for. However, I am also committed to constantly listening, learning, and un-learning - work that, in my opinion, is never done. Your voice is integral to what we do as a student union. I'm running to make sure students are consistently informed, engaged, and no one is left behind.

What I Stand For

Student Engagement and Consultation

Establish a formal consultation process.

The VP Academic advocates for student interests on all matters relating to UBC - but we don't have a formal consultation process with students and community members.

If elected, one of my priorities will be to establish clear guidelines for consulting equitably with students and community members when advocating to UBC on major decisions, policies, and projects.

Senate also needs a formal consultation process and guidelines - similar to the Board of Governors Engagement Charter.

Launch Student Engagement Campaign

In my first 2 years at UBC, the words "Board of Governors" or "Senate" had no meaning to me. UBC has a complicated and bureaucratic system of governance - one that can be difficult to navigate for the average student.

As your VP Academic and Senate representative, I will push for a Student Engagement Campaign to raise awareness on what these bodies do, how their policies affect you, and how you can get involved and make your voice heard. This includes all things AMS as well - transparency and accountability are key to rebuilding trust with the student body and are integral to our mission as a student union.

Transparency and Accountability

Prioritize Transparency and Accountability

Very few students (23%) agree that the University has been transparent and communicative about where student tuition is going towards.

When it comes to AMS representing student financial interests, students continue to be more likely to disagree (33%) than agree (27%) as well as when trusting AMS to spend their money wisely (37% disagree, 25% agree).

Transparency on the university's financial matters will be a huge priority for me - while simultaneously ensuring the student body knows exactly where their money is going for the AMS.

Affordability


Tuition and Learning Materials

UBC voted to increase tuition last year during a pandemic, despite over 90% of students saying they opposed increases and without a long-term student affordability plan.

If elected, I will continue to oppose tuition increases without a long-term Student Affordability Plan from UBC and better tuition consultation processes.

Thankfully, this work is getting started with the Student Affordability Taskforce - however this is a slow process and this issue needs the urgency it deserves. Students are struggling and need support now.

We need better tuition consultation, following current recommendations from the SATF.

We need more transparency with where our finances are going - especially when it comes to important issues such as the climate emergency - it’s time that UBC puts their money where their mouth is.

According to the 2021 Academic Experience Survey, the average amount spent on textbooks was $893, with a median of $400.Continue advocating for the use of Open Educational Resources (OERs) in all faculties and courses to alleviate financial burdens on students.

Housing

Advocate for UBC to prioritize building more affordable and sustainable student housing as part of Campus Vision 2050.

Vacancies in Vancouver have gone down and rent has gone up - housing in UBC often has extremely long waitlists and is one of the only affordable options available to students.

The average rent for a two-bedroom unit in a purpose-built rental building climbed last year to $1,824, while the average rent in a two-bedroom condo was up to $2,498.

Addressing Food Insecurity

40% of students at Canadian universities are experiencing some degree of food insecurity, 4x higher than the general Canadian population.

According to the 2021 AES, only around 10% of all UBC students have used the food bank. However, 42% of all students have been concerned at some point regarding financial resource to purchase food.

In line with the Food Security Initiative:

Push for more sustainable, affordable, healthy, and culturally diverse solutions to food insecurity on campus - including in residences.

Continue to support and partner with Sprouts Café, UBC Food Hub, Fooood outlets, Acadia Hampers, and Agora

Allow students to donate their unused Residence or Flex Dollars to students-in-need as part of UBC's Meal Share Program.

Accessibility and Safety

Accessibility

According to UBC's Wayfinding program, 49 buildings out of the 189 on campus are inaccessible. Of the 140 remaining, 50 have no information on accessibility.

Get UBC to commit to establishing an accessibility taskforce to address accessibility issues - this can be through a task force between the BoG and Senate.

Invest in making buildings accessible, as well as improving the Wayfinding Interface. Mandate regular accessibility audits

We currently have commitments to universal accessibility in the Vancouver Campus Plan - this has to be a priority in Campus Vision 2050, our land-use plan for the next 30 years.

“To ensure the equal participation by people of all abilities in the future, The Campus Plan goes beyond basic compliance with accessibility standards. It seeks to create a barrier-free campus by encouraging the application of universal design principles in planning and designing new facilities, major renovations and retrofits to facilities and the public realm.”

Campus Vision 2050: “Academic building space that supports a range of flexible teaching and learning environments, including hybrid in-person and online formats.”

Accessibility has to be looked at holistically and in all forms. This includes physical and learning spaces. Having the option of flexible and hybrid learning ensures that students can access an education - especially during a pandemic where students may be at higher risk, immunocompromised, or at risk of passing COVID-19 to those at risk.

Campus Safety

As we return to in-person instruction, it is vitally important that everyone feels safe on our campus.

According to our Academic Experience Survey, a majority of students have experienced discrimination on campus and 20% of students who have experienced sexual assault or another form of sexual misconduct during their time at UBC.

In the 2021 AES, there were also significant declines in agreement that UBC has clearly communicated resources regarding sexual violence.

The Sexual Assault Support Centre has seen a huge increase in the use of their services in the past few years - this will most likely continue to rise as we have more students on campus and more social events and parties occurring.

It is vital that students have access to these kinds of support services - a reason why I am 100% in support of the SASC fee increase this year.

If elected, I will work closely with the SASC not only on student support, but also on education and prevention, in line with our current commitments in I-17.

Community grassroots efforts such as the SASC are essential to the UBC community - it's about time we acted as such.

Campus security has regular anti-oppression and sexual violence training.

COVID-19 and student health

Continue supporting students through the pandemic

Continue to advocate for students who are immunocompromised, disabled, and at-risk.

Continue pushing for flexible, hybrid, and multi-access learning.

Ensure there is more open communication and increased transparency between UBC and the student body regarding decisions related to COVID-19

UBC did not communicate to the students that they had purchased N-95s

Late communication on extending online school

Those living in residence were generally unaware of the testing and quarantine requirements and options available to them

Advocate for expanded rapid testing on campus for asymptomatic and symptomatic individuals and establish a pick-up location for rapid tests in the Nest

Mental Health Services

Continue to advocate for student mental health supports, ensuring that IBPOC receive culturally competent and trauma-informed mental health services.

Continue to advocate for mandatory mental health training for UBC staff, faculty, AND student leaders.

Continue to partner with groups like the Social Justice Center to hold regular drug testing services and distribute testing strips and naloxone kits.

Push UBC to invest in long-term harm reduction services for students, including a permanent testing clinic and launching a de-stigmatization campaign for substance use on campus.

Section 3.10 of Year Round Residence Contract - Any involvement, whether direct or indirect, in any illegal drug or drug-related activity is prohibited. For greater certainty, being present where others are consuming illegal drugs or engaging in illegal drug related activity is not permitted—if others are engaging in this activity you must leave. Possession of paraphernalia that is associated with the possession, use or trafficking of illegal drugs is prohibited. These activities may result in eviction and referral to the police.

Indigenous Initiatives

Center Indigenous voices and expand Indigenous initiatives

Decolonization should be central to everything we do at the AMS - this starts with uplifting and centering Indigenous students and voices.

Currently, we have only one position within the AMS dedicated to Indigenous engagement and initiatives - the Indigenous Engagement Facilitator.

If the AMS is truly committed to supporting and uplifting Indigenous voices and issues, we must expand this position to at least two part-time staff, while providing more support and resources to these positions.

Consultation and engagement

Ensure Indigenous students are engaged properly in academic policies and AMS advocacy, in line with the AMS Equity Action Plan and UBC's Indigenous Strategic Plan, in a way that is trauma-informed and not extractive or tokenizing.

Prioritize the Indigenous Strategic Plan in all Senate efforts and advocacy

As per Goal 4 in the ISP, advocate to include Indigenous ways of knowing, culture, histories, experiences and worldviews in curriculum delivered across Faculties, programs and campuses.

Continue advocating for Indigenous students, faculty, and staff recruitment and retention, while prioritizing Indigenous presences in campus planning and Campus Vision 2050.

Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion

Ensure the AMS and UBC are internally equitable.

Equity, Diversity, and Inclusion are central to all of my commitments - it is vitally important that we recognize that these issues are all interconnected with issues of accessibility, affordability, climate justice, etc.

As leaders, we have a duty to be sufficiently trained in anti-racism, anti-oppression, consent, and sexual violence education.

Advocate for mandated anti-racism and consent education for all AMS leaders and staff as well as UBC leaders, staff, and faculty

Work with the VP Administration to establish a centralized database for all EDI workshops/resources available on campus for students and clubs.

Advocate for an AMS Standing Committee on EDI, instead of a sub-committee, in line with the Equity Action Plan

Climate Justice


Hold UBC accountable to the Climate Action Plan

Ensure UBC is following through on their commitments to the Climate Action Plan (CAP 2030) and make their financial commitments transparent with the UBC community and student body.

Representatives from UBC Media Relations told The Ubyssey in fall 2020 that UBC invests “tens of millions of dollars into climate operations and research,” but did not provide further details. The Ubyssey reached out to the President’s Office last November asking about UBC’s process for funding climate projects, but received no response.

UBC's latest budget says the university has invested an “extra” $1.3 million toward climate action projects in the 2021/22 school year, but did not specify where the money went. The university-backed Sustainability Hub noted that some projects have funding amounts listed in its Annual Sustainability Report, but could not provide further information.

Ensure the effects of climate change and sustainable land use is prioritized in UBC's land-use plan, Campus Vision 2050

Ensure IBPOC concerns and voices, as well as the Indigenous Strategic Plan, are central to climate advocacy at UBC.
